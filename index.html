Moroccan Soul

History & Culture

A land defined by its "Maghreb" identity—where the sun sets—Morocco offers a timeline spanning from Roman ruins to French protectorate chic. The culture is rooted in Amazigh (Berber) traditions and Islamic scholarship, most evident in the preserved labyrinths of the old cities.

Cuisine & Etiquette

Expect vibrant flavors of saffron, cumin, and ginger. Etiquette: In January, as you move through local souks, a "Salam Alaykum" (peace be upon you) is the standard greeting. It is customary to use only the right hand for eating and gesturing.

January Guidance

Weather from Jan 17–24 is the peak of Moroccan winter. Days are bright and solar-warmed (18°C), but as soon as the sun dips, temperatures plummet. Bring a versatile trench or wool coat for the Fes and Desert evenings.
